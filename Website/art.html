Art

Art is a HUGE part of my life! It was my first real interest and has gone on to play an enormous role in my career choice, hobbies and even who I am as a person.I'm going to introduce you to some of the first artists I learnt about and began to inspire me:

---------------------

William Blake

William Blake (28 November 1757 – 12 August 1827) was an English poet, painter, and printmaker. Largely unrecognised during his lifetime, Blake is now considered a seminal figure in the history of the poetry and visual arts of the Romantic Age.

"Nebuchadnezzar" (1757-1827)

"The Ghost Of a Flea" (1757-c1827)

"God Judging Adam" (1795)

---------------------

JMW Turner

Joseph Mallord William Turner (23 April 1775 – 19 December 1851), known contemporarily as William Turner, was an English Romantic painter, printmaker and watercolourist. He is known for his expressive colourisations, imaginative landscapes and turbulent, often violent marine paintings.

"The Burning of the Parliament Houses" by JMW Turner (1834-5)

"Peace Burial at Sea" by JMW Turner (1842)

"Snow Storm: Hannibal and his Army Crossing the Alps" by JMW Turner (1812)

---------------------

Roy Lichtenstein

Roy Fox Lichtenstein (; October 27, 1923 – September 29, 1997) was an American pop artist. During the 1960s, along with Andy Warhol, Jasper Johns, and James Rosenquist among others, he became a leading figure in the new art movement. His work defined the premise of pop art through parody.

"Whaam!" by Roy Lichenstein (1963)

"Modern Art I" by Roy Lichenstein (1996)

"Reflections on Girl" by Roy Lichenstein (1923-1997)
